01 — Overview

Bidding Player is a drop-in JavaScript tag that lets a publisher monetize their video inventory with real-time header bidding without writing any Prebid integration code.

A single <script> tag, configured with data-* attributes, handles everything:

  • Lazy-loads video.js, Google IMA SDK, and a custom Prebid build (≈ 250 KB total, parallelized).
  • Registers an instream video ad unit with the limelightDigital bidder.
  • Runs the auction against your configured timeout (default 1200 ms).
  • Stitches the full Prebid targeting key-value set (hb_pb, hb_bidder, hb_uuid, hb_size, hb_format, hb_adid, plus bidder-suffixed variants) into your GAM master ad tag's cust_params.
  • Hands the final VAST URL to IMA, which serves either the winning bid's cached creative (resolved via hb_uuid against Prebid Cache) or your house ad if no bid wins.

The engine is hosted at versioned, immutable jsDelivr URLs. A tag pinned to @v1.2.2 serves the exact bytes that were tested at release time and cannot silently change.

What's bundled

  • video.js v8 (player UI)
  • Google IMA SDK v3 (ad rendering)
  • Prebid.js v11.11.0 with the limelightDigital adapter, TCF v2 / USP consent modules

What you provide

  • A Limelight publisher ID + ad unit ID
  • Your GAM master video ad tag
  • GAM line items targeting hb_* keys
  • The content video URL

02 — How it works

The end-to-end auction-to-impression sequence:

Browser loads the publisher page; the engine's async <script> executes.
Engine reads its own data-* attributes for runtime configuration.
Engine parallel-loads video.js, IMA SDK, and Prebid.js from CDN.
Engine measures the mount-div's rendered size; registers an instream video ad unit.
Prebid → Limelight: POST OpenRTB request to {host}/ortbhb.
Limelight → Prebid: bid response (or no-bid).
Prebid caches the winning VAST at prebid.adnxs.com/pbc/v1/cache; receives a UUID.
Engine calls pbjs.getAdserverTargetingForAdUnitCode to obtain the full hb_* key set.
Engine applies its floor bias to hb_pb; stitches all keys into GAM's cust_params.
Engine → IMA: AdsRequest.adTagUrl = <stitched GAM URL>.
IMA → GAM: fetches VAST; matches your line item targeting hb_pb.
GAM returns a VAST wrapper whose URL contains {hb_uuid}; GAM substitutes the macro with the cached bid's UUID.
IMA → Prebid Cache: resolves the wrapper, retrieves the actual bidder VAST.
IMA plays the ad. Engine pauses content (z-index swap on ad container).
Ad completes; Engine swaps z-index back; content resumes.
Fallback path

If Prebid fails to load, the bidder times out, or no bid comes back, the engine still calls GAM with hb_pb=0.00&hb_bidder=none in cust_params. GAM's house ad / passback line item fills the impression. The publisher never loses an ad opportunity to a network failure.

03 — Prerequisites

Before integrating, confirm you have all of the following:

RequirementWhere to get it
Google Ad Manager account with video inventory enabled admanager.google.com
GAM Master Ad Tag URL for the video slot (must end with correlator=) GAM → Inventory → Ad units → "Generate tags"
Limelight Digital publisher account (publisher ID + ad unit ID) Limelight account management
Limelight allowlist approval for your domain(s) Submit via your Limelight account manager. 1–3 business days typical.
HTTPS publisher pages (mixed content blocks IMA + bidder calls) Your hosting
Content video URL (.mp4 or .m3u8) Your CDN
Allowlist check

The most common cause of no bid response in production is the publisher's domain not being on Limelight's allowlist. Confirm allowlist status with Limelight before launching, otherwise every auction will return no-bid and you'll think the integration is broken.

04 — Quick start

Five steps from zero to a live monetizing player.

  1. Generate your tag

    Open the Tag Generator at aryanvani-pm.github.io/bidding-player and fill in:

    • Release version (use the latest tag, e.g. v1.2.2)
    • Your GAM Master Ad Tag URL
    • Your Limelight Publisher ID and Ad Unit ID
    • Your content video URL

    Click ⚡ Generate Production Tag and copy the resulting <script> snippet.

  2. Set up GAM line items + creative

    Create one VAST wrapper creative whose URL is your Prebid Cache + {hb_uuid} macro, and one price-bucket line item per $0.10 CPM step. See section 06 for the exact configuration. (You only need to do this once per ad unit.)

  3. Embed the snippet

    Paste the generated <script> tag into your page at the position where the video player should render. The engine auto-creates the mount <div> next to the script.

  4. Smoke-test in your browser

    Open the page with ?debug=true appended to the URL. In DevTools → Console you'll see STEP 1, STEP 1.5, then either WINNER or NO MARKET DEMAND, then STEP 6. In the Network tab, filter for ortbhb to see the OpenRTB bid request.

  5. Monitor

    Once live, monitor in GAM Reports for hb_bidder=limelightDigital impressions, eCPM, and fill rate. If you don't see Limelight impressions after 24 hours, see section 10.

05 — Embed code reference

The canonical embed snippet. Every configurable knob is a data-* attribute on the <script> tag.

<script src="https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/aryanvani-pm/bidding-player@v1.2.2/engine/player.js"
        id="adtech-player-core"
        data-pub-id="649658371"
        data-adunit-id="972556929"
        data-tag="https://pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/ads?iu=/...&sz=640x480&output=vast&correlator="
        data-timeout="1200"
        data-bias="0.10"
        data-video="https://your-cdn.example.com/content.mp4"
        data-autoplay="true"
        data-muted="true"
        data-fluid="true"
        data-loop="false"
        data-preload="metadata"
        data-vpaid="insecure"
        data-div-id="comparos-video-placement"
        data-host="ads-jbi003.rtba.bidsxchange.com"
        data-cache="https://prebid.adnxs.com/pbc/v1/cache"
        data-prebid-url="https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/aryanvani-pm/bidding-player@v1.2.2/prebid/prebid.js"
        async></script>

Attribute reference

Attribute Required Default Description
src Yes — Versioned jsDelivr URL of the engine. Always pin to a tag (@v1.2.2), never @main.
id Yes adtech-player-core Must equal adtech-player-core. The engine uses this ID as a fallback for legacy browsers.
async Yes — Required HTML attribute. The engine boots after the DOM is ready and won't block page rendering.
data-pub-id Yes — Your Limelight publisher ID.
data-adunit-id Yes — Your Limelight ad unit ID (numeric).
data-tag Yes — Your full GAM Master Ad Tag URL. Must end with correlator= (no value) so the engine refreshes it per request.
data-prebid-url Yes — URL of the Prebid.js build. Must include limelightDigitalBidAdapter.
data-host No ads-jbi003.rtba .bidsxchange.com Limelight bidder endpoint host. Only change if Limelight directs you to.
data-video No — Content video URL (.mp4 / .m3u8). If omitted, only the pre-roll plays.
data-timeout No 1200 Prebid bidder timeout in milliseconds. Industry default for video is 1500–2000.
data-bias No 0.10 Floor bias in USD added to the winning CPM before bucketing.
data-autoplay No true Browser autoplay policy requires muted=true for unattended autoplay.
data-muted No true Whether the player starts muted. Required if autoplay=true in most browsers.
data-fluid No true true = responsive 16:9. false = fixed 640×480.
data-loop No false Loop the content video after it ends.
data-preload No metadata HTML5 video preload hint: metadata, auto, or none.
data-vpaid No insecure IMA VPAID handling: insecure, enabled, or disabled.
data-div-id No comparos- video-placement ID of the mount div. If no element exists at boot, the engine creates one.
data-cache No prebid.adnxs.com /pbc/v1/cache Prebid Cache server. Switch to your own for higher SLA and volume.
Don't change versions piecemeal

The src and data-prebid-url attributes must always point at the same version tag. The Tag Generator enforces this; if you hand-edit your snippet, keep them in sync.

06 — GAM setup

This is the side that handles the actual ad serving. It requires careful one-time configuration of custom key-values, a VAST wrapper creative, and price-bucket line items.

Step 1: Create custom key-values

In GAM go to Inventory → Key-values → New key-value. Create the following:

Key nameValue typeSuggested values
hb_pbPredefined0.00, 0.10, 0.20, …, 20.00 (one per $0.10 step)
hb_bidderPredefinedlimelightDigital, none
hb_uuidFree-form(UUIDs are dynamic)
hb_sizePredefined640x360, 1280x720, 1920x1080
hb_formatPredefinedvideo
hb_adidFree-form(dynamic)
hb_sourcePredefinedclient, s2s

Step 2: Create the VAST wrapper creative

The creative is a single VAST URL with a macro that pulls the cache UUID at serve time:

https://prebid.adnxs.com/pbc/v1/cache?uuid=%%PATTERN:hb_uuid%%

The %%PATTERN:hb_uuid%% macro is GAM's syntax for "look up the hb_uuid key-value from the ad request and inject its value here."

To create:

  1. GAM → Delivery → Creatives → New creative
  2. Select your video ad unit's advertiser
  3. Type: Video → VAST 3.0 ad tag
  4. Paste the URL above into the VAST tag URL field
  5. Set duration to 0:00
  6. Save

Step 3: Create price-bucket line items

You need one line item per $0.10 price bucket from $0.10 up to your maximum expected CPM (e.g. $20.00 = 200 line items). The structure is identical for each.

Per line item

FieldValue
NamePrebid HB $2.50 (or your naming convention)
Inventory sizeVideo — match your ad unit's size(s)
TypePrice Priority
CPM rateMatch the bucket exactly (e.g. $2.50)
GoalNone (unlimited) or your impression cap
Targeting → InventoryYour video ad unit
Targeting → Key-valueshb_pb is one of 2.50
CreativeThe VAST wrapper from Step 2

Use Prebid's official Line Item Generator tool (docs.prebid.org/tools/line-item-generator.html) to bulk-create all 200 line items via CSV upload — manual creation is impractical at this volume.

Exact-match values matter

Prebid's hb_pb values are always strings like "2.50" (two decimal places, no currency symbol). Your GAM line item targeting must match that exact string. A line item targeting "2.5" will not match "2.50" and will never serve.

Step 4: Set up house ad fallback

When no bid wins, the engine sends hb_pb=0.00&hb_bidder=none. Create one of:

  • A house line item targeting hb_pb=0.00 with a low priority and your fallback creative.
  • A remnant line item targeting the same key-value pointing at AdSense / a tag-based ad network.
  • Leave it blank if you're OK with the slot serving nothing on no-bid (rare; most pubs want to fill 100%).

Step 5: Wrap line items in an Order

GAM requires every line item to live inside an Order. Create a single Order called e.g. "Prebid Header Bidding" and assign all 200 price-bucket line items + the fallback to it. This lets you pause / activate the entire integration with one click.

07 — Limelight onboarding

The bidder side is owned by Limelight Digital. Steps you (or your account manager at the platform) follow:

  1. Apply for a Limelight account. If you don't have one, contact your platform's CSM to request publisher onboarding.
  2. Receive your credentials. Limelight provides:
    • publisherId — numeric, e.g. 649658371 → goes into data-pub-id
    • adUnitId — numeric, e.g. 972556929 → goes into data-adunit-id
    • host — bidder endpoint, e.g. ads-jbi003.rtba.bidsxchange.com → goes into data-host
  3. Submit your domain(s) for allowlist. Limelight only bids on requests from approved publisher domains. Without this step, every auction returns no-bid in production.
  4. Wait for allowlist approval. Typically 1–3 business days.
  5. Verify bidding is live using the steps in section 09.
Tip

You can test the integration mechanics (script loads, auction fires, GAM call goes out, ad plays from GAM's test creative) before your domain is allowlisted. The bidder side will return no-bid until allowlisting completes — that's why the engine has a fallback path.

08 — Consent management

The engine ships consent-aware defaults appropriate for non-EU traffic but requires an override for EU / California / other-state-privacy traffic.

What the engine does by default

FrameworkDefault behavior
GDPR (TCF v2)Declares gdprApplies: false via static config. The bundled tcfControl module skips enforcement. Suitable for US/India/non-EU traffic.
USP (CCPA)Auto-detects window.__uspapi with a 100 ms timeout. If no CMP responds, proceeds without USP signal.
GPP (US state privacy)Not activated. The bundled GPP module cancels the auction outright when no GPP CMP is found, so it is intentionally left off.

EU publishers — enable real TCF v2 consent

If you serve EU traffic, place a registered TCF v2 CMP on your page (OneTrust, Sourcepoint, Quantcast, Didomi, etc.) and override the engine's static GDPR config. Place this snippet before the engine's <script> tag:

<script>
window.pbjs = window.pbjs || { que: [] };
pbjs.que.push(function() {
  pbjs.setConfig({
    consentManagement: {
      gdpr: {
        cmpApi: 'iab',        // detect window.__tcfapi
        timeout: 8000,         // wait up to 8s for CMP response
        defaultGdprScope: true  // assume GDPR applies unless CMP says otherwise
      }
    }
  });
});
</script>

California / Connecticut / Virginia (USP)

The engine auto-detects USP. Ensure your CMP exposes window.__uspapi. To extend the timeout:

pbjs.setConfig({
  consentManagement: {
    usp: { cmpApi: 'iab', timeout: 3000 }
  }
});

Multi-state US (GPP)

If your CMP supports GPP, opt in explicitly:

pbjs.setConfig({
  consentManagement: {
    gpp: { cmpApi: 'iab', timeout: 3000 }
  }
});
Without a CMP in regulated jurisdictions

Operating in the EU, California, or other regulated states without a registered CMP is a regulatory risk independent of this player. The engine's permissive defaults are a publisher-experience choice — they do not constitute legal compliance. Consult counsel.

09 — Testing & verification

Before declaring the integration live, verify each of the following.

Enable debug mode

Append ?debug=true to your page URL. The engine logs the auction flow in the browser console with color-coded steps.

Console checklist

Open DevTools → Console. Filter for STEP. You should see:

  • STEP 0.5 Mount point auto-created (only if you didn't pre-place the div)
  • STEP 1 Auction Initialized via CDN. Target: limelightDigital | Timeout: 1200ms
  • STEP 1.5 Requesting video ad payload. Player size: XXX×YYY
  • Either WINNER <bidder> | Raw CPM: $X.XX (real bid) or NO MARKET DEMAND
  • STEP 3 Math Engine processing (only on winning bid)
  • STEP 6 Dispatching VAST Request via Cloud Engine layer

Network checklist

Open DevTools → Network. Verify each of these requests fires:

cdn.jsdelivr.net/.../prebid/prebid.js200Prebid bundle loaded
imasdk.googleapis.com/.../ima3.js200IMA SDK loaded
POST {host}/ortbhb200OpenRTB bid request fired. Inspect Payload tab.
prebid.adnxs.com/pbc/v1/cache200Caches the winning VAST (only on winning bid)

Visual checklist

  • The video player renders at the expected size and position on the page.
  • A pre-roll ad plays before content.
  • Content video plays after the ad completes.
  • Standard Video.js controls (play/pause, mute, volume, progress, fullscreen) appear and respond.

10 — Troubleshooting

No POST /ortbhb request in the Network tab

Means Prebid never fired the bidder. Possible causes:

  • Adapter not in Prebid build. Inspect pbjs.installedModules in the console — limelightDigitalBidAdapter must be present.
  • Consent module blocking the auction. Look for "Canceling auction as per consentManagement config" in console.
  • Adapter rejected the ad unit. Look for auctionDebug messages: "video ad unit containing bidders that don't support video" means your Prebid build is missing the adapter.
POST /ortbhb returns 200 but bid response is empty

Bidder responded but didn't bid. Almost always:

  • Domain not allowlisted by Limelight. Verify with Limelight that your origin is on the approved list.
  • Floor below Limelight's threshold. If you've set a floor, lower it.
  • Ad slot size not supported. If the player is unusually small (< 300×200), bidders may not bid.
Bid wins but no ad plays / wrong ad plays / GAM falls through to house

The auction worked but GAM failed to resolve the bidder's creative. Check:

  • The GAM URL cust_params must contain hb_uuid. If missing, you're on an old engine version (< v1.2.0) — upgrade.
  • Your line item targeting must match hb_pb exactly. Prebid emits "2.50", your line item must target "2.50", not "2.5".
  • Your VAST wrapper creative must use %%PATTERN:hb_uuid%%. Confirm in GAM → Delivery → Creatives.
  • Your Prebid Cache must be reachable from the user's browser.
Request timeout after 1200ms in console

Bidder didn't respond in time. Options:

  • Increase data-timeout to 1500 or 2000 ms.
  • Check Limelight's regional endpoint — ask if they have a closer host.
Ad plays but content doesn't resume after

IMA's CONTENT_RESUME_REQUESTED event didn't fire or was caught by a publisher CSS rule. Check that the mount div has no parent rule forcing pointer-events: none or opacity: 0 on the ad container.

Player doesn't autoplay

Browser autoplay policies require muted=true for unattended autoplay. Confirm:

  • data-autoplay="true" AND data-muted="true" are both set.
  • The page is served over HTTPS.
  • The user hasn't disabled autoplay globally in their browser settings.

On iOS Safari, additional restrictions apply — there is no workaround at the engine level.

Console error: currentScript missing anchor validation

The engine couldn't locate its own <script> tag. Ensure the snippet has id="adtech-player-core". Regenerate the tag if necessary.

Mixed content warnings in the console

Your page is HTTPS but one of these URLs is http://: data-video, data-tag, or a creative returned by Limelight/GAM. The browser will block these. Force all URLs to HTTPS.

12 — FAQ

Can I run multiple players on the same page?

Not in the current engine. The integration assumes a single video ad unit per page. Workaround: load the engine in an iframe per player.

Can I add other Prebid bidders besides limelightDigital?

You need a custom Prebid build that includes the additional adapters. The engine currently registers only one bidder per auction. Adding more bidders requires an engine code change — talk to your account manager.

What happens if Prebid fails to load?

The engine catches the failure and calls GAM directly with hb_pb=0.00&hb_bidder=none. Your house ad / fallback line item fills the impression.

How do I track auction performance?

Impression-level metrics (eCPM, revenue, fill rate) are available natively in GAM Reports — filter by the hb_bidder key-value. For auction-level metrics (bid latency, bid rate), request a Prebid build with an analytics adapter.

Will ad blockers affect this?

Yes, like all Prebid integrations. Common ad blockers will block the engine script, prebid.js, the bidder endpoint, the GAM call, IMA SDK, and the resulting ad. The engine has no special evasion behavior.

What's the latency impact on my page?

The engine script is async and doesn't block page rendering. The auction adds ~1200 ms (the configured timeout) before the first ad starts playing. Zero impact on Time to Interactive; a configurable delay only on the pre-roll ad start.

Can I customize the player controls or skin?

The player is video.js v8 with the default skin. Override video.js CSS by placing rules after the engine's <script> tag, targeting .video-js selectors.

How often does the player refresh the auction?

Once per page load. There is no automatic refresh. Mid-roll ads are not currently supported.

What's the difference between @v1.2.2 and @main?

@v1.2.2 is an immutable tag — the URL is cached aggressively (1-year CDN TTL) and serves the exact bytes from release time. @main points at the latest commit, cached ~12 hours. Use @main for development; always pin to a version tag for production.

Is this GDPR / CCPA compliant out of the box?

The engine includes the technical hooks for compliance. Whether your overall integration is compliant depends on placing a registered CMP on your page in regulated jurisdictions, your privacy policy disclosures, and your data processing agreements with bidders. Consult counsel.
